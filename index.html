PRNG Implementation

1. Definition & Purpose

A Pseudo-Random Number Generator (PRNG) is an algorithm that produces a sequence of numbers that approximates the properties of random numbers. Unlike true random number generators, a PRNG is deterministic; if you start it with the same initial value (called a seed), it will always produce the same sequence. The purpose of a PRNG is to generate seemingly random data for various applications, from scientific simulations to video games and cryptography.

2. Strengths and Weaknesses

Linear Congruential Generator (LCG)

Strength: Extremely fast and simple to implement. Requires minimal memory, making it ideal for resource-constrained environments.

Weakness: The output sequence is predictable and has a short period. Not suitable for cryptographic applications due to its lack of security.

Blum Blum Shub (BBS)

Strength: A cryptographically secure PRNG (CSPRNG). Its security is based on the computational difficulty of factoring large numbers, making its output unpredictable.

Weakness: Extremely slow compared to LCG and other modern PRNGs. Requires large prime numbers and significant computational resources, limiting its practical use.
